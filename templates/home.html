A GATEWAY TO SCIENCE
The Runnymede STEM Club is an organisation founded and solely led by students in which we share our scientific knowledge with younger children from many backgrounds in the form of fun, challenging and interactive experiments. Our goal has always been to impart our passion for the sciences, especially physics, that us Sixth Formers have onto younger students, especially girls.
The gender gap in higher-level STEM classes is still real and tangible today. In the most advanced maths courses at our school, men dominate 13:1. In physics this is 9:1. We founded the STEM club to foster enthusiasm for science and technology equally in girls and boys, and believe the key to combating gender expectations and limitations at the root is to target younger students. In light of this, we carry out weekly practicals in school for 8- to 12-year-old students, allowing them to taste experimental science first-hand at earlier stages in school than would be available otherwise. We are also committed to spreading a love for science to disadvantaged communities, so we visit a charity to do experiments with underprivileged teens. Because we hold that this effort should be pursued years into the future, we train students to continue the club, teaching them to give back to their community and participate in progress, one person at a time.
